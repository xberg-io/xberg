Introduction
Caffeine (1,3,7-trimethylxanthine) is a stimulant of the central nervous system.
This review examines the evidence for both beneficial and adverse effects of caffeine consumption.
Methods
Search Strategy
    <p>We searched MEDLINE, Embase, and the Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials.</p>

    <sec id="s4">
      <title>Discussion</title>
      <p>Our findings suggest that caffeine has complex effects on human health.</p>
      <p>Low to moderate consumption may provide cognitive benefits <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="ref1">1</xref>, while high doses may lead to adverse effects <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="ref2">2</xref>.</p>
    </sec>
    <sec id="s5">
      <title>Conclusions</title>
      <p>Caffeine consumption should be tailored to individual tolerance and health status.</p>
