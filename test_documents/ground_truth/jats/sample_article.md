# Effects of Caffeine on Human Health

## Abstract

### Background

Caffeine is one of the most widely consumed psychoactive substances in the world.

### Methods and Findings

We conducted a systematic review and meta-analysis of studies examining the effects of caffeine on human health.

### Conclusions

Moderate caffeine consumption appears to have both beneficial and adverse health effects.

## Introduction

Caffeine (1,3,7-trimethylxanthine) is a stimulant of the central nervous system.

This review examines the evidence for both beneficial and adverse effects of caffeine consumption.

## Methods

### Search Strategy

We searched MEDLINE, Embase, and the Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials.

### Selection Criteria

We included randomized controlled trials and observational studies examining caffeine effects.

## Results

We identified 47 studies meeting inclusion criteria.

## Characteristics of Included Studies

Summary of study design and population characteristics.

## Discussion

Our findings suggest that caffeine has complex effects on human health.

Low to moderate consumption may provide cognitive benefits 1 , while high doses may lead to adverse effects 2 .

## Conclusions

Caffeine consumption should be tailored to individual tolerance and health status.
