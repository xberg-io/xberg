Dear Jody Pellissey,

The Délinę Renewable Resources Council (DRRC) has concerns regarding the joint proposal by
Environmental and Natural Resources (ENR) and Tłįcho Government (TG) entitled "*Revised Joint
Proposal on Management Actions for Wolves (diga) on the Bathurst and Bluenose-East Barren-ground
Caribou (zekwo) Herd Winter Ranges: 2021-2024.*"

For many years now, Déliņę and other Sahtú communities have been expressing their concerns regarding
diga (wolf) culls to ENR and co-management bodies including the Sahtú Renwable Resources Board
(SRRB) and the Wek'èezhii Renewable Resources Board (WRRB). At the 2016 SRRB hearing on
Bluenose East, Elders from all 5 Sahtú communities described the important relationship between díga
and zekwę (caribou). The Elders explained that the diga keep the zekwę populations healthy, that killing
all the diga might actually wipe out the zekwę, and that the diga must be respected. This relationship is
also described in Déliņę's Belarewıle Gots'ę ?ekwę, or caribou conservation plan, which was formally
approved as a community conservation plan by the SRRB and was accepted and endorsed by the Minister
of the Environment and Natural Resources in 2016.

The DRRC is concerned that ENR and TG's joint proposal has not considered the perspective of Sahtú
Dene Elders. The DRRC does not believe that aerial shooting is respectful of the diga, and is concerned
that this approach will not protect ?ekwę. In addition to being opposed to this specific method of díga
culls, the DRRC is opposed to any díga culls occurring in or near the overlap area between the Déliņę and
WRRB boundaries.

The DRRC requests information on where the díga culls will be occurring and what impact they will have
on diga within the Déliņę area specifically and within the Sahtú region generally. The DRRC requests that
the WRRB hold a full hearing to ensure that there is sufficient consultation on this matter of great
importance to the Délinę Got'inę.
