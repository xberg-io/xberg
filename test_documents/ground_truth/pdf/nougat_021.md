## Re: 2021-2024 Diga Revised Joint Management Proposal

Dear Jody Pellissey,

The Délinç Renewable Resources Council (DRRC) has concerns regarding the joint proposal by Environmental and Natural Resources (ENR) and Tłicho Government (TG) entitled "Revised Joint Proposal on Management Actions for Wolves (diga) on the Bathurst and Bluenose-East Barren-ground Caribou (żekwę) Herd Winter Ranges: 2021 – 2024."

For many years now, Délinç and other Sahtú communities have been expressing their concerns regarding diga (wolf) culls to ENR and co-management bodies including the Sahtú Renewable Resources Board (SRRB) and the Wek'eezhli Renewable Resources Board (WRRB). At the 2016 SRRB hearing on Bluenose East, Elders from all 5 Sahtú communities described the important relationship between diga and żekwę (caribou). The Elders explained that the diga keep the żekwę populations healthy, that killing all the diga might actually wipe out the żekwę, and that the diga must be respected. This relationship is also described in Délinç's Belarewile Gots' ę Żekwę, or caribou conservation plan, which was formally approved as a community conservation plan by the SRRB and was accepted and endorsed by the Minister of the Environment and Natural Resources in 2016.

The DRRC is concerned that ENR and TG's joint proposal has not considered the perspective of Sahtú Dene Elders. The DRRC does not believe that aerial shooting is respectful of the diga, and is concerned that this approach will not protect ?ekwé. In addition to being opposed to this specific method of diga culls, the DRRC is opposed to any diga culls occurring in or near the overlap area between the Délíne and WRRB boundaries.

The DRRC requests information on where the diga culls will be occurring and what impact they will have on diga within the Délíne area specifically and within the Sahtú region generally. The DRRC requests that the WRRB hold a full hearing to ensure that there is sufficient consultation on this matter of great importance to the Délíne Got'ine.
