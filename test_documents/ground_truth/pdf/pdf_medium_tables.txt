Abstract

Background: Patients infected with hepatitis C virus (HCV) genotype 1 respond poorly to standard treatment with
peginterferon-α and ribavirin. Some studies show that genetic polymorphisms in the promoter region of IL28B gene is
predictive for treatment response. The effect of core amino acid substitutions (genetic variations) on treatment outcome,
and the impact of the core region on treatment outcome was explored in this study.

Methods: We resequenced HCV treatment-tailored samples (obtained from 95 Swedish adult peginterferon
alpha/ribavirin-treated HCV genotype 1 infected patients) for viral core, NS3 and NS5A regions. We used direct sequencing
to examine core mutations and IL28B polymorphisms. We compared mutations found in patients with good
versus poor treatment response.

Results: The association between treatment response and substitutions of core residue 91 was found to correlate
with the clinical response. Of the core samples in 6/37 (15%) of poor responders versus 0 in 14 (0%) of good
responders with SVR, SVR and SVR. Of the 37 strains that had ILl28B (C/C in 5/37 (14%) of poor responders and 0
in 14 (0%) of good responders. Nor one substitution mutation that was found to be protective. Core 70
substitution and IL28B CT genotype predicted lower SVR.

Conclusions: The results indicate that substitutions of core residue 91 are related to treatment response in
Swedish patients with HCV in patients, but not all test mutations predict treatment response.
